Constellation Observer 2.0

The Story

This project is a tribute to my Dad's invention, a chair to help observe the stars. My Dad in the early 90s invented a "chair" to help observe the stars. It was a car seat on top of a metallic structure with some rotors and a stand for binoculars, so the user can just be seated holding a remote to control the chair and watch the stars. He used to show this in public schools as a science project but you can't observe stars at day time so he added some early type of goggles that connect to a laptop/pc and used a constellation program so you could simulate watching the night sky indoors.

Enter the Star Field

How to Use

You can look around by moving your device. For a more immersive experience, use a VR headset like Google Cardboard. You can also connect a Bluetooth joystick to your phone to control the view.

Access on your Phone

Scan this QR code with your phone's camera to open the app directly.
